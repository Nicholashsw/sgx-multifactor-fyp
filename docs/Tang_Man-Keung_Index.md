## His IMF Working Papers (sole author or first author)

### Tang, M.-K. (2007), *Private-Sector Financial Liabilities in Advanced Economies: Is More Better?* — IMF Working Paper WP/07/118

- **Landing page:** https://www.imf.org/en/publications/wp/issues/2016/12/31/private-sector-financial-liabilities-in-advanced-economies-is-more-better-20941
- **SSRN:** https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=989432
- **Summary:** Cross-country, cross-industry analysis of how household and corporate borrowings affect economic outcomes since 1995. Key finding: the type of financial system (bank-based vs market-based) and labor market flexibility moderate the impact of expanded credit on real activity.
- **Why relevant to you:** Foundational for thinking about credit-cycle effects in your independent macro research macro scenarios. Connects to the dot-com bubble work you've been doing on PFI.

### Tang, M.-K. and Yu, X. (2011), *Communication of Central Bank Thinking and Inflation Dynamics* — IMF Working Paper WP/11/209

- **Landing page:** https://www.imf.org/en/publications/wp/issues/2016/12/31/communication-of-central-bank-thinking-and-inflation-dynamics-25147
- **SSRN:** https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=1923523
- **Summary:** How central bank communication about its own economic assessment shapes inflation dynamics. Estimates a simple monetary model on Mexican data and uses it to illustrate scenario-dependent responses to shocks.
- **Why relevant to you:** Directly relevant to MAS communication analysis. Also useful for thinking about policy-uncertainty shocks in your local projections work.

### Lee, J. and Tang, M.-K. (2003), *Does Productivity Growth Lead to Appreciation of the Real Exchange Rate?* — IMF Working Paper WP/03/154

- **Landing page:** https://www.imf.org/en/Publications/WP/Issues/2016/12/30/Does-Productivity-Growth-Lead-to-Appreciation-of-the-Real-Exchange-Rate-16630
- **SSRN:** https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=879230
- **Summary:** Revisits the Balassa-Samuelson productivity-RER channel. Counter-intuitive finding: higher *total factor* productivity may lead to RER *depreciation*, with the appreciation channel operating mainly through tradable prices rather than non-tradable inflation.
- **Why relevant to you:** Directly informs how you think about SGD strength under productivity shocks, a recurring theme in macro strategy at independent macro research.

### Terrier, G., Valdés, R., Tovar, C. E., Chan-Lau, J. A., Fernández-Valdovinos, C., Garcia-Escribano, M., Medeiros, C. I., **Tang, M.-K.**, Vera-Martin, M., Walker, W. C. (2011), *Policy Instruments to Lean Against the Wind in Latin America* — IMF Working Paper WP/11/159

- **Landing page:** https://www.imf.org/en/publications/wp/issues/2016/12/31/policy-instruments-to-lean-against-the-wind-in-latin-america-25024
- **SSRN:** https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=1888902
- **Summary:** Multi-author survey of macroprudential tools used in Latin America: capital requirements, dynamic provisioning, loan-to-value caps, reserve requirements, capital flow taxes. Tang is one of ten authors.
- **Why relevant to you:** Useful taxonomy of macroprudential instruments. Lower priority than the three above because he's one author of many.
